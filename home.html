What is Mardi Gras

Mardi Gras is French for "Fat Tuesday," a reference to the fact that Mardi Gras always is the day before Ash wednesday, the beginning of Lent in the Catholic Faith. Becasue of the 40 days of Lent are considred a somber period of sacrifice, Fat Tuesday is seen by many as an appropraite time for indulgence and festivity- a last chance at reverlry. Nowhere in the United States is Mardi Gras celebrated as it is in Louisiana.
